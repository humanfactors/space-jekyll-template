About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Western Australia in the Human Factors Laboratory. My research project investigates the cognitive mechanisms that underlie performance in safety-critical complex jobs, such as air traffic control, with a particular focus on interruption, workload, and prospective memory. I have a strong interest in reproducible research methods and statistics, and recently I have been applying Bayesian methods and reproducible research practices to my own work. Currently, I am trying to focus my free time on GNU/Linux, "big data", and machine-learning. Feel free to get in contact with me or read more about my services.
